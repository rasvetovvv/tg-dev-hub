# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Telegram users browsing a single developer's catalog of projects, scripts, and source code — almost always on a phone, inside the Telegram WebApp, often mid-conversation. A secondary user is the admin (the developer) who publishes projects and runs the monetization side: tiers, promo codes, subscriptions, VIP, custom-dev requests. The job to be done: quickly find a useful project, understand what it is and what access it needs, and download or buy it without friction.

## Product Purpose

A catalog + lightweight storefront for developer assets. It exists so one developer can distribute and monetize their work directly through Telegram: free/paid/subscription/VIP access tiers, daily download limits for free users, promo codes, "buy source", and "order customization" requests. Success = a visitor lands, grasps the catalog in seconds, and reaches a download or purchase with minimal taps.

## Brand Personality

Developer-native and terminal-confident: precise, technical, unpretentious, with a single warm amber phosphor accent against deep cool ink. Three words: **precise, technical, warm**. It should feel like a well-made CLI tool, not a marketing site — earned familiarity over spectacle.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS cream/pastel landing-page aesthetics.
- Over-animated marketing motion (orchestrated page-load sequences, decorative parallax).
- Glassmorphism-as-default, gradient text, hero-metric template.
- Anything that makes a focused tool feel like a brochure.

## Design Principles

1. **Earned familiarity.** Behave like the tools developers already trust; the interface disappears into the task.
2. **Density with clarity.** Mobile-first and compact, but every element reads as deliberate — never cramped, never a wall of chrome.
3. **Identity through accent and type, not chrome.** The amber accent and the mono/display/body type system carry the brand; decoration does not.
4. **Motion conveys state.** Animation exists for feedback, transitions, and reveals — never decoration. Sub-300ms, ease-out, reduced-motion always honored.
5. **One vocabulary everywhere.** Same button shapes, form controls, badges, and icon family across catalog, drawer, account, and admin.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA for text contrast (including placeholders), visible keyboard focus on every interactive element, 44×44px minimum touch targets (primary surface is a phone), and a `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback for every animation. Honor Telegram theme/safe-area where applicable.
